"Strawberry Rhubarb Jam"

Backstory

Strawberry Rhubarb Jam is one of the best seasonal recipes from the summer, in my opinion. Rhubarb will grow for you 3 times, sometimes even 4 times a season. Be sure to take advantage of this summertime boon. Your second harvest should line up with strawberry picking season, at least it does for those in the northeast United States.

Ingredients

  • 2 quarts of Fresh Strawberries
  • 12 stalks of Rhubarb
  • 3/4 cup Brown Sugar
  • 1 cup White Sugar
  • 1 tsp Salt, any kind will do
  • 2 tsp Ginger root powder
  • 3 tsp Cassia or Ceylon Cinnamon
  • 4 cups of water
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice

Cookware and Utensils Required

  • Medium-Large Pot
  • Service spoon or ladle, ideally wooden
  • Chef or Pairing knife
  • Spoon to test the tack

Steps

  1. Wash and dry produce.
  2. Slice strawberries and dice rhubarb.
  3. Place rhubarb, salt, and water in a pot and bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  4. Add 3/4 cup of brown sugar, reduce heat to medium.
  5. Cook for 30 minutes, stirring every minute or so.
  6. Add strawberries, ginger, cinnamon, white sugar, and lemon juice.
  7. Reduce liquid by half, then turn heat to low.
  8. For storage, follow canning instructions and prepare mason jars.
  9. Store in the fridge, it won't last long enough to be perishable.
